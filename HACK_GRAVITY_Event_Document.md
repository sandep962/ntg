## 2. PROBLEM TRACKS

Explore our four cutting-edge tracks. Choose the domain that ignites your passion.

### Track 1: AI & Machine Learning
**Description:** Harness the power of artificial intelligence to build intelligent systems that can learn, predict, and automate.
**Problem Statements:**
1.  **Next-Gen accessible Education:** Build an AI-driven personalized tutoring system that adapts to the learning disabilities and pacing of individual students in rural areas.
2.  **Predictive Healthcare Diagnostics:** Create an ML model that analyzes preliminary patient data to predict the early onset of lifestyle diseases with high accuracy.
3.  **Fake News & Deepfake Detection:** Develop an automated pipeline that can verify the authenticity of multimedia content spread across social media networks.
*Best for:* Data Science enthusiasts, ML engineers, Python wizards, and NLP researchers.

### Track 2: Web3 & Blockchain
**Description:** Build decentralized, transparent, and secure applications for the future of the internet.
**Problem Statements:**
1.  **Transparent Supply Chains:** Design a blockchain solution to trace agricultural products from farm to table, ensuring fair pay for farmers and standard quality for consumers.
2.  **Decentralized Identity Verification:** Create a zero-knowledge proof (ZKP) based seamless identity verification system for accessing financial services without compromising privacy.
3.  **Tokenized Climate Action:** Implement a decentralized platform for issuing, trading, and burning tokenized carbon credits for small to medium-sized enterprises.
*Best for:* Solidity developers, cryptography enthusiasts, Web3 architects, and Rust developers.

### Track 3: Climate & Sustainability
**Description:** Engineer technological solutions to combat climate change, reduce waste, and build a sustainable future.
**Problem Statements:**
1.  **Smart E-Waste Exchange:** Build a platform connecting consumers with certified e-waste recyclers, optimizing logistics and incentivizing safe disposal.
2.  **Hyper-Local Grid Optimization:** Develop a system that balances local renewable energy generation (like rooftop solar) with neighborhood consumption to minimize grid reliance.
3.  **Precision Agriculture Dashboard:** Create a tool that integrates satellite imagery and IoT sensor data to help farmers optimize water and fertilizer usage.
*Best for:* IoT engineers, full-stack developers, mapping/GIS enthusiasts, and green-tech innovators.

### Track 4: Open Innovation
**Description:** No limits. No boundaries. Solve a profound real-world problem of your choosing that doesn't fit into the other three tracks.
**Problem Statements:**
1.  **FinTech & Financial Inclusion:** Build tools that grant underserved demographics access to modern banking, credit, and investment.
2.  **Urban Mobility & Smart Cities:** Design traffic management, public transit tracking, or parking optimization software for congested Indian metros.
3.  **Mental Health & Well-being:** Create an anonymous, accessible, and gamified support platform for college students facing academic burnout.
*Best for:* Product visionaries, creative developers, UI/UX masters, and out-of-the-box thinkers.

## 4. JUDGING CRITERIA

### ROUND 1 — Online Idea Submission
Your Idea Deck (PPT/PDF file format) will be evaluated out of 100 points:
*   **Originality & Innovation (30 Points):** Is the idea fresh, unique, and creative?
*   **Problem-Solution Fit (30 Points):** Does the solution effectively and realistically solve the stated problem?
*   **Technical Feasibility (20 Points):** Can this idea be built into a working prototype in 24 hours?
*   **Clarity of Presentation (20 Points):** Is the deck professional, clear, and visually appealing?
*The top 40 cumulative scores globally will earn an exclusive invite to the offline finals.*

### FINAL ROUND — Offline (Top 40 Teams)
Judges will explicitly score prototypes on the following 6 criteria (Total: 100%):

1.  **Technical Complexity & Implementation (25%):**
    *   *What judges look for:* Quality of the code, integration of complex APIs/algorithms, lack of mock data, and overall engineering excellence.
2.  **Innovation & Creativity (20%):**
    *   *What judges look for:* How out-of-the-box the solution is. Does it challenge the status quo?
3.  **UI/UX & Design (15%):**
    *   *What judges look for:* Smooth user flows, aesthetic appeal, intuitive design, and accessibility.
4.  **Impact & Scalability (15%):**
    *   *What judges look for:* How wide the real-world impact could be. Is the business model viable? Can the tech handle millions of users?
5.  **Completeness of Prototype (15%):**
    *   *What judges look for:* Does the demo actually work? Are there broken links or crashing modules during the presentation?
6.  **Presentation & Pitch (10%):**
    *   *What judges look for:* Team confidence, storytelling ability, and clarity in answering judges' questions.
    *   *Format:* 5 Minutes Live Demo + 3 Minutes Rapid Q&A.
